<p><p>Fresnel lenses are classified into two main market types: imaging Fresnel lenses and non-imaging Fresnel lenses. Imaging Fresnel lenses are designed to focus or magnify light, making them suitable for applications like projectors, camera lenses, and solar concentrators. On the other hand, non-imaging Fresnel lenses are primarily used for light diffusion or light concentration, commonly seen in applications such as lighting fixtures, traffic signals, and optical systems that require wide-angle illumination. Both types serve different purposes and find applications across various industries based on their specific optical properties.</p></p>
