In the casting market, aluminum alloy wheels are produced by pouring molten metal into a mold, allowing for complex designs and cost-effective production. Conversely, the forging market involves shaping the wheels by applying heat and pressure to remove excess material and enhance strength. The "other market" encompasses various manufacturing techniques like spinning or machining, which offer specific advantages such as improved durability or reduced weight.</p></p>
