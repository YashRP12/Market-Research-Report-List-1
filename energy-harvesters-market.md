<p><p>Energy harvesters refer to devices designed to capture and convert various forms of ambient energy into usable electrical power. Vibrational energy harvesters convert mechanical vibrations into electrical energy, typically found in machinery or infrastructure. Electromagnetic energy harvesters utilize magnetic fields to generate power from moving parts or electrical induction. Solar/light harvesters harness sunlight or artificial light to generate electricity. Wind/fluidic harvesters capture wind movements or fluid flows for energy generation. Other types of energy harvesters include thermal, piezoelectric, and radiofrequency, all converting specific ambient energies into usable power sources.</p></p>
