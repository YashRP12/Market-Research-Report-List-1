<p><p>PETG shrink film is a type of plastic film made from polyethylene terephthalate glycol, a thermoplastic polymer. It is commonly used in the packaging industry to wrap products and provide them with protection and an attractive appearance. PETG shrink film has excellent clarity, high strength, and good resistance to chemicals and moisture, making it suitable for various applications such as food packaging, pharmaceuticals, beverages, and consumer goods.</p><p>The PETG shrink film market is expected to experience significant growth in the coming years. The global demand for pet packaging films is increasing due to the rising consumption of packaged food and beverages, as well as the growing e-commerce industry. PETG shrink films are preferred by packaging manufacturers for their versatility, high shrinkage ratio, and ability to conform to irregular shapes, ensuring the pack's tight fit.</p><p>Additionally, the demand for sustainable packaging solutions is driving the market further. PETG shrink films are recyclable, reducing environmental impact and aligning with the growing consumer preference for eco-friendly packaging options. As sustainability becomes a priority for companies and consumers, the adoption of PETG shrink films is likely to increase.</p><p>The market is also influenced by technological advancements in shrink films, including increased barrier properties, improved printability, and enhanced sealability. The second type consists of films with a TD value between 60% and 70%, representing moderate impact resistance. These categories are used to assess and classify the varying strength and durability of PETG shrink films available in the market.</p></p>
