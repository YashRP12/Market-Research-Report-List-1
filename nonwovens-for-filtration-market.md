<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>Nonwovens for filtration market types include airlaid, wetlaid, spunlaid, meltblown, needlepunching, hydro-entanglement, and thermobonded. Airlaid method involves a high-pressure airflow to distribute and bind fibers. Wetlaid uses a water-based suspension to form a web. Spunlaid method uses extruded filaments that are laid down randomly and thermally bonded. Meltblown involves the extrusion of molten polymer to create fine fibers. Needlepunching uses barbed needles to mechanically entangle fibers. Hydro-entanglement uses high-pressure water jets to entangle fibers. Thermobonded involves heating and binding of fibers. These techniques offer diverse options for producing nonwoven filtration materials.</p></p>
