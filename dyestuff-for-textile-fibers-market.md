<p><strong>The Dyestuff for Textile Fibers Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Disperse Dyes</li><li>Reactive Dyes</li><li>Sulfur Dyes</li><li>Vat Dyes</li><li>Acid Dyes</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The dyestuff for textile fibers market can be categorized into various types. Disperse dyes are suitable for synthetic fibers like polyester and are dispersed in water during the dyeing process. Reactive dyes chemically react with the fiber and form a strong covalent bond, mainly used for cellulosic fibers like cotton. Sulfur dyes have high wash and light-fastness, making them ideal for dyeing cotton and other natural fibers. Vat dyes are insoluble in water but become soluble through a reduction process, commonly used for cotton fibers. Acid dyes are used for protein fibers like wool and silk, while others include basic dyes and direct dyes.</p></p>
