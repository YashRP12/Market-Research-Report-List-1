<p><p>Energy-efficient windows are designed to minimize heat transfer and maintain a comfortable indoor temperature, thus reducing energy consumption. Double glazing windows consist of two glass panes separated by a layer of air or gas, providing better insulation than single glazing. Triple low-E glazing, on the other hand, adds a third glass pane with a low-emissivity coating, further reducing heat loss and increasing energy efficiency. Both types of windows offer significant energy-saving benefits and help to reduce heating and cooling costs for homeowners and businesses.</p></p>
