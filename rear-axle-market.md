<p><p>The rear axle market comprises three main types: drive, dead, and lift axles. Drive axles are responsible for delivering power to the wheels and are commonly used in vehicles that require torque and traction, such as trucks. Dead axles, on the other hand, do not provide power to the wheels but support the vehicle's weight, typically found in trailers. Lift axles are an additional set of wheels that can be raised or lowered, offering increased load-carrying capacity or improved maneuverability in situations where additional traction or weight limits are needed.</p></p>
