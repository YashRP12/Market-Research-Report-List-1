<p><p>Dough conditioners and additives are substances used in the baking industry to improve the quality and texture of dough. There are two main types: powders and fluids. Powdered dough conditioners typically come in a dry form and are added directly to the dough mixture. They provide stability, elasticity, and better volume to the dough. On the other hand, fluid dough conditioners are in liquid or gel form and can be easily mixed into the dough. They enhance the dough's texture, flavor, and overall appearance. Both types of conditioners and additives play a vital role in creating high-quality bakery products.</p></p>
