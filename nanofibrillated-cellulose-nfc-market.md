<p><p>Nanofibrillated Cellulose (NFC) is a type of cellulose material that is produced by mechanically disrupting cellulose fibers, resulting in nanoscale fibrils. Nanocrystalline Cellulose (NCC) is another type of cellulose material, but it is produced by breaking down cellulose into crystalline particles. Bacterial Nanocellulose, on the other hand, is produced by certain bacteria that create cellulose in the form of nanofibers. These materials have various applications in industries like packaging, textiles, and composites, due to their exceptional strength, high surface area, and biodegradability.</p></p>
