<p><ul><li>Air Drying</li><li>Freeze Drying</li></ul></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>Dehydrated mushroom flakes are available in two market types: air drying and freeze drying. Air drying involves extracting moisture from mushrooms by exposing them to air at a specific temperature and airflow. This method is cost-effective and commonly used for commercial production. On the other hand, freeze drying involves rapidly freezing mushrooms, followed by a process of sublimation where ice converts directly into vapor, resulting in minimal loss of flavor, texture, and nutrients. While more expensive, freeze drying is favored for its superior quality retention and shelf stability.</p></p>
