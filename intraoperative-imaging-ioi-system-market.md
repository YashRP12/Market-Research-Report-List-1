<p><p>Intraoperative imaging (IOI) systems are advanced medical devices used during surgeries to provide real-time images for physicians. There are three main types of IOI systems: intraoperative CT, intraoperative MRI, and intraoperative ultrasound. Intraoperative CT uses computed tomography technology to capture detailed images of the patient's body during the surgery. Intraoperative MRI employs magnetic resonance imaging to produce high-resolution images, while intraoperative ultrasound utilizes sound waves to generate real-time images that help guide surgical procedures. These IOI systems enable surgeons to enhance precision and accuracy during surgeries, leading to improved patient outcomes.</p></p>
