<p><p>SiC devices are categorized based on their voltage ratings, which determine their applications and capabilities. High-voltage SiC devices are designed to handle high electrical potentials, typically above 1,200 volts, and are used in applications such as power transmission, renewable energy systems, and electric vehicles. Medium-voltage SiC devices have voltage ratings between 600 and 1,200 volts, suitable for applications like industrial motor drives and grid-level energy storage. Low-voltage SiC devices operate below 600 volts and find applications in consumer electronics, electric power supplies, and server power systems.</p></p>
