<p><p>CMOS analog switches are electronic components that control the flow of signals in electronic circuits. The SPST (Single-Pole Single-Throw) type refers to switches that have one input terminal and one output terminal, allowing the connection or disconnection of a single signal path. On the other hand, the SPDT (Single-Pole Double-Throw) type has one input terminal and two output terminals, enabling the switching between two signal paths. Both types are widely used in various applications, including audio/video systems, communications, data acquisition, and instrumentation.</p></p>
