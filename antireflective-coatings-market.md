<p><p>Different types of antireflective coatings are used in the market. Vacuum deposition is a process in which a thin layer of coating material is deposited onto the substrate inside a vacuum chamber. Electronic beam evaporation involves the use of an electron beam to heat the coating material and vaporize it onto the substrate. Sputtering is a technique in which ions are accelerated towards a target material, causing the release of atoms that form a thin film on the substrate. These methods are commonly used to apply antireflective coatings on various surfaces, reducing reflections and enhancing optical performance.</p></p>
