<p><p>Hydrogen sensors for the automotive market are classified into four types. Pressure sensors detect changes in hydrogen pressure, ensuring safe operation and avoiding pressure-related accidents. Temperature sensors detect hydrogen temperature, facilitating efficient fuel cell operation. Hydrogen exhaust sensors monitor the hydrogen concentration and detect potential leaks. Mass air flow sensors measure the amount of hydrogen entering the combustion engine, optimizing fuel efficiency. All these sensors play crucial roles in mitigating risks, enhancing performance, and improving the overall safety and efficiency of hydrogen-powered vehicles. They are fundamental components for the development and advancement of the automotive industry's hydrogen technology.</p></p>
