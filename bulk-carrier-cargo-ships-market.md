<p><ul><li>Geared bulk carriers</li><li>Combined carriers</li><li>Gearless carriers</li><li>Self-dischargers</li><li>Lakers</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Bulk carrier cargo ships can be categorized into different market types based on their features and capabilities. Geared bulk carriers are equipped with onboard cranes, allowing them to load and unload cargo independently. Combined carriers have both gearless holds and equipped holds for enhanced flexibility. Gearless carriers lack onboard cranes and rely on external facilities for cargo handling. Self-dischargers have conveyor systems that enable them to unload cargo quickly. Lakers market refers to bulk carriers operating in the Great Lakes region of North America, which have specific size restrictions due to the waterway's navigational constraints.</p></p>
