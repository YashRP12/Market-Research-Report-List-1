<p><p>Semiconductor bonding wax is available in two market types, solid and liquid. Solid bonding wax is in a solid form at room temperature and needs to be melted before use. It is commonly used for temporary bonding and can be easily removed by heating. On the other hand, liquid bonding wax is already in a liquid state and can be applied directly without any prior melting. It is often used for permanent bonding and offers greater convenience. Both solid and liquid bonding waxes are utilized in the semiconductor industry for various bonding applications.</p></p>
