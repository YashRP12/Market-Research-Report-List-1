<p><p>The spin-on glass process refers to a method of depositing a thin layer of glass onto a substrate by spinning the substrate at high speeds. The market for spin-on glass processes can be broadly classified into two types: hot-temperature spin process and normal spin process. The hot-temperature spin process involves depositing the glass layer at high temperatures, typically above 450 degrees Celsius, enabling better adhesion and planarization. On the other hand, the normal spin process is performed at lower temperatures, typically below 350 degrees Celsius, suitable for applications where high-temperature processing is not feasible.</p></p>
