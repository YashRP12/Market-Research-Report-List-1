<p><p>Opto-isolators, also known as optocouplers, are electronic devices that use light to transfer electrical signals between two isolated circuits. They consist of a light-emitting diode (LED) on one side and a phototransistor or photodiode on the other side, separated by a transparent medium. When an electrical signal is applied to the LED, it emits light which activates the phototransistor or photodiode, thereby allowing the signal to be transferred without any direct electrical connection.</p><p>The opto-isolator market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. The increasing demand for electric vehicles, industrial automation, and renewable energy systems is driving the market growth. Opto-isolators are widely used in these applications to provide electrical isolation and protect sensitive electronic components from voltage spikes and surges.</p><p>Moreover, the growing trend of miniaturization in electronics and the need for high-speed data transmission is also fueling the demand for opto-isolators.
