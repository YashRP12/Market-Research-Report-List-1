<p><p>Pure biodiesel, also known as B100 or neat biodiesel, is a renewable form of diesel fuel that is produced from vegetable oils or animal fats through a process called transesterification. Vegetable oil is the dominant feedstock segment, primarily due to the easy availability of feedstock such as soybean oil, palm oil, and canola oil.</p><p>The transportation sector is the largest application segment for pure biodiesel, driven by the increasing demand for biofuels in the automotive industry. Power generation is also a significant application segment, as biodiesel can be used in generators and boilers.</p><p>In terms of geography, North America is the largest market for pure biodiesel, owing to supportive government policies and the presence of a well-established distribution network.
