<p><p>Quicklime, also known as calcium oxide (CaO), is a widely used chemical compound derived from limestone. It is produced by heating limestone at high temperatures, which drives off carbon dioxide, leaving behind the quicklime. It has various applications across industries such as construction, agriculture, steel manufacturing, and water treatment.</p><p>The market for quicklime is expected to showcase significant growth in the coming years. The construction industry is a major consumer of quicklime, as it is used in mortar and plaster applications. With rapid urbanization and increasing construction activities worldwide, the demand for quicklime is expected to rise. Additionally, the growing usage of steel in infrastructure projects drives the demand for quicklime as a fluxing agent, further fueling market growth.</p><p>The agriculture industry also contributes to the demand for quicklime due to its use in soil stabilization and neutralization. Quicklime helps in reducing soil acidity and improving nutrient availability, promoting enhanced crop production. As agricultural practices are becoming more advanced and focused on sustainable farming, the demand for quicklime is projected to increase.</p><p>Furthermore, the water treatment industry is a key end-user of quicklime. Quicklime is widely employed in the treatment of wastewater and as a disinfectant due to its alkaline nature. With increasing concerns over water pollution and the need for clean water resources, the demand for quicklime in water treatment applications is anticipated to grow substantially.</p><p>The market outlook for quicklime is positive, with a projected CAGR of 10.8% during the forecasted period.
