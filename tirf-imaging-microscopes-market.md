<p><p>TIRF imaging microscopes are used to observe and study biological samples with high resolution. There are two main types of TIRF imaging microscopes based on the market variations: the prism type and the objective lens type. Prism type TIRF microscopes use a prism to create the evanescent wave, allowing only the fluorophores close to the surface to be illuminated. On the other hand, objective lens type TIRF microscopes use specific objective lenses to generate the evanescent wave, enabling the observation of the illuminated region without the use of a prism.</p></p>
